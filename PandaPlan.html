Overview

Redesigning When2Meet to an entirely new website, to try and reach its full potential.

Working in a team, we wanted to create a better version of When2Meet by focusing on the aesthetics of the site. We would use and consider some of the website aspects as is, but add some aspects of our own as well. The idea was to create a whole new website, our own version of When2Meet.




The Problem

When2Meet is a scheduling tool website that is meant to aid people with different time schedules, find availability with a combined time schedule. However, the current version of When2Meet feels old and has a lot of room for improvement.


The Solution

Use When2Meet and compare it to other scheduling websites. We tried to incorporate some outside ideas that would make sense with a scheduling website. Also, it was important we take note of the aesthetics for users. After conducting some research, we knew we had to incorporate the following changes:

  • Change the current aesthetics of the website (color, font, etc.)
  • Have a home page for events
  • Add an event and member page
  • Add more ways to share events (i.e. text messaging)



The Design Process

1. User Research

Our main method of user research was a survey. The survey was aimed towards finding the current likes/dislikes of When2Meet. Other research conducted was based on our own personal knowledge of previous classes, such as functional requirements of the website and use case diagrams.




2. Data Analysis

Survey insights:

When it came to the login process and sense of reliability from the website, people had no problem with these two aspects. However, when it came to the layout of the website users would agree that fonts/colors could be improved on the site. Along with that, the methods used to input individual schedules could be improved. The overall processes that the website had users involved in, were easy enough to understand.

Takeaways

This was my first redesign project and my first project course overall. All in all, I believe my group and I did good considering that we came up with a whole new product. If we had more time, I'm sure we could've conducted more research methods and more design concepts. Overall, the experience I gained during this time was worth-while; it only gets better from here.

In the future, I would like to take this project further and conduct much more research than what we did here. I would definitely incorporate some interviews in the project, as well as having some users test the product for aesthetics. I believe part of having a successful product is having good aesthetics for users.
