Overview

Redesigning When2Meet to an entirely new website, to attempt to reach its full potential.

Working in a team, we wanted to create a better version of When2Meet given that as it stands, it could benefit from a redesign. We wanted to primarily focus on the aesthetics of the site, since most of the siteâ€™s functionalities were heading in the right direction. Going into it, we had a strong sense that what drove users away the most was its visuals; it was old and unappealing, almost skeptical in a way. In the end, the idea was to create a whole new website, our own version of When2Meet.

The Problem

When2Meet is a scheduling tool that takes in time schedules from a group of people and produces one big, combined schedule. Using this, people can specifically view what time slots all the people in their group are available. As previously mentioned, although the concept and functionality of this website is good, the current version of When2Meet feels outdated and has a lot of room for improvement.

The Solution

Use When2Meet concurrently with other scheduling websites and compare their usability. We would then incorporate ideas we believed worked well with a scheduling website, as well as take note of the aesthetics. Much of the decisions we made were based on user-research and after gathering some evidence, we concluded that we needed to incorporate the following changes:

  • Change the current aesthetics of the website (color, font, etc.)
  • Include a home page for different events
  • Aggregate an event and member page, that lists the different members and events a user has
  • Extend the capability for sharing events (i.e. text messaging)
The Design Process

1. User Research

Our main method of user-research was a survey. The survey was aimed towards discovering what the current likes/dislikes of When2Meet were. Other research conducted was based on our own personal knowledge from previous courses, such as functional/nonfunctional requirements for the website and use-case diagrams.

2. Data Analysis

Survey insights:

When it came to the login process and a sense of reliability from the website, people had no problem with these two aspects. However, when it came to the layout of the website users agreed that fonts/colors could be improved. Along with that, the methods used to input individual time schedules had room for improvement. The overall processes users were involved with, were easy enough to understand.

3. Low-Fidelity
Wireframes

After conducting user research and gathering data, we decided to make our first few wireframes of the website. These low-fidelity wireframes incorporated a big change in aesthetics, as well as our own personal ideas. We created our own logo and added new pages aside from When2Meet's default ones. This is what we believed the functional prototype should look like in the end.

Takeaways

This was my first redesign project and my first project course overall. All in all, I believe my group and I did good considering the project time and scope we were given. In the end, we came up with some sort of visuals using the research data we were able to gather. If we had more time, I'm sure we would've conducted new research methods to gather more data and propose new design concepts as well. Overall, the research and design experience I gained during this time was worth-while; it only got better from here.

In the future, I would like to take this project further and conduct much more research than what was done here. Additional to that, I would also like to create a functional prototype of the project. I would definitely incorporate some interviews in the project, as well as having some users test the product for aesthetics. I believe the success factor for this project is having good aesthetics.
